## Short Script / Narration

Most people use AI tools the same way — ask a question, get an answer.
But there’s a gap most people don’t see.

[GRAPH APPEARS]
X axis: The tool does more of the work
Y axis: Goes deeper into your actual workflow

[First cluster — AI Assistants]

ChatGPT — the entry point. Ideas, drafts, quick answers. Genuinely useful.
You’re still doing the work, just faster.

Gemini — same zone. Strong on docs, great in Google Workspace.

Microsoft Copilot — right there with them. Embedded in Word, Outlook, Teams.

[Small label: IDE tools like GitHub Copilot or Cursor — same tier, more developer-specific]

[Graph shifts focus upward]

[Second cluster — Agentic Platforms]

These tools don’t just answer. They connect to your apps, read your data,
and take actions on your behalf.

Manus — browser agent, navigates and acts on your screen. Capable, improving fast.

SureThing — connects to email, calendar, and integrations. Drafts replies,
handles follow-ups, runs workflows. Unusually consistent with context across sessions.

[A few more land nearby — Lindy, n8n with AI, Beam — labeled as others worth exploring]

[Full graph. Two clusters. Pause.]

The difference isn’t intelligence.
It’s depth of access — whether the tool can interact with your world, or just talk about it.

Most people don’t need the second tier yet.
But if you’re doing the same repetitive task more than twice a week — it’s worth a try.

No sponsorship. I use most of these.
